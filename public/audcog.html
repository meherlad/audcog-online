The AudCog study aims to understand why hearing loss is linked to cognition in health and disease.

We are using new tests of listening to understand how the brain processes sound.

To learn more about this study or to take part click on the links above or scroll below.

The AudCog sub-study of PREVENT consists of 3 hearing tests and a questionnaire. [ VIDEO ]

1. The Digits in Noise test

We will test you ability to identify numbers on a background of static noise. You will be played 3 numbers and then be asked to click on the numbers you were played in the right order. We are interested in what your limit for doing this is, so the test will adapt to you. If you are finding it easier at the beginning, then the computer will make it harder for you until it finds your limits. It is completely normal to be guessing on trials at some points. There is no 'correct' level of performance.
[ VIDEO ]

2. The Speech in Babble test

We will test your ability to pick out 5 words on a background of people talking. You will be played 5 words and then be asked to click on the words you were played from a list on the screen. We are interested in what your limit for doing this is, so the test will adapt to you just like the test above. If you are finding it easier at the beginning, then the computer will make it harder for you until it finds your limits. It is completely normal to be guessing on trials at some points. Again, there is no 'correct' level of performance.
[ VIDEO ]

3. The Auditory Precision test

We will test your ability to match the pitch of tones and the speed of another sound (like a moving train) precisely. In this task, you will be played a sound and then asked to keep it in mind for a few seconds. You will then be shown a horizontal scale that plays different sounds on it at different locations. For example, if you are asked to keep a tone in mind, clicking on the left half of the scale plays lower pitch tones that the right of the scale. For the train-like sounds, clicking on the left part of the scale would then play 'slow train' sounds and clicking in the right part of the scale would play 'fast train' sounds. Each trial is self-paced and you can click as many times as you want. Over time you will get better at this task and that is what we are interested in
[ VIDEO ]

4. The Musical Sophistication questionnaire

This questionnaire will measure your 'musical behaviours'. You will be asked to pick how strongly you agree with questions about various aspects of musical behaviours in your life. Try to answer it to the best of your ability. Also, think about a time just prior to the coronavirus pandemic for questions relating to event attendances.
[ QUESTIONNAIRE ]

Videos will guide you through each test and the questionnaire will show questions in between the tasks.
